Play Office 2023–2025

Impact

Since launching in 2023, Play Office has built free creative technology infrastructure for Portsmouth — connecting artists, communities, funders and cultural partners.

Overview Since 2023

Play Office is a free creative technology lab, equipment library and artist studio in Portsmouth. Since launching, Play Office has engaged 40 artists, reached 2,996 project participants, welcomed 3,472 exhibition visitors and delivered 13 education events for 356 attendees.

Through free workshops and shared equipment access, Play Office has provided the equivalent of £16,200 in public education value and helped reduce the need for individual equipment purchases. Play Office is also home to Resonate, an artist-development programme that has supported 12 alumni, paid £30,000 in artist fees and helped artists tour work across 5 countries.

Play Office operates as civic creative technology infrastructure: a free equipment library, artist development platform, workshop space, international exchange hub and public programme for immersive media, heritage and community-led digital storytelling.

All services are free or low-cost, with access for artists, community groups, students and cultural organisations in Portsmouth and beyond.

Resonate — Artist Development 2023–2025

Resonate programme outcomes

Resonate is Play Office's artist development programme for artists experimenting with immersive media, XR, digital storytelling and new forms of public engagement.

By the end of Resonate II, all tracked artist-development indicators had reached the maximum rating. Resonate II artists showed an average 113% progression across tracked development indicators, including sector awareness, professional networks, funding confidence and XR integration confidence.

Resonate artists have gone on to tour work internationally, win commissions, develop new practices and gain further funded opportunities directly as a result of programme participation.

Equipment library & education 2025

Free equipment library

Through partnership with Share Portsmouth and the Library of Things, Play Office's free equipment library has saved £2,120 in equipment purchases and an estimated 0.3 tonnes of carbon through shared access to creative technology.

The library provides access to VR headsets, projection equipment, sound tools, sensors, 3D capture devices and immersive media hardware — free for artists, students and community groups.

Education and workshops

Play Office has delivered 13 education events attended by 356 people, providing £16,200 of equivalent workshop value free to the public. Sessions have covered VR, projection mapping, photogrammetry, 3D scanning, animation, XR storytelling and digital heritage.

Play Office has supported 8 successful Arts Council England-funded programmes, and raised £23,083 through British Council-supported international exchange towards future collaborations.
