Accessibility Statement

Play Office is committed to making its space, website and programmes accessible to as many people as possible. This page explains what you can expect when visiting or participating in Play Office activities.

If you have access requirements not covered here, please contact us before visiting or attending a session and we will do our best to support you.

The venue

Play Office is located at The Old Pay Office, Portsmouth Historic Quarter, Victory Gate, HM Naval Base, Portsea, Portsmouth PO1 3LJ.

The venue is within the historic dockyard estate. Please contact us for the most up-to-date information about step-free access routes, as some areas of the site are subject to ongoing change.

Step-free access

Play Office is step-free accessible. If you have specific mobility requirements or would like to discuss access arrangements before visiting, please email us and we will make sure your visit or session works for you.

Toilets

Toilet facilities are available within the Portsmouth Historic Quarter site. Please contact us for the most up-to-date information on accessible toilet locations.

Transport and parking

The site is accessible by foot, bicycle and car from central Portsmouth. There is limited parking on and around the historic dockyard site. For travel planning, we recommend using the Traveline journey planner.

The nearest bus stops are on Queen Street and The Hard. Portsmouth Harbour railway station is approximately 10 minutes on foot.

Workshops and events

We aim to make all Play Office workshops and events inclusive. Where possible, we can offer:

  • Seated participation in hands-on workshops
  • Quieter start times or pre-session visits for participants who need to familiarise themselves with the space
  • Clear written instructions alongside verbal explanations
  • Carer or companion attendance at no extra cost
  • Flexible timing for participants who need breaks

If you have specific requirements for a workshop or event, please contact us before booking and we will discuss how we can support you.

Sensory environment

Some Play Office workshops and events involve audio, projection, flashing lights or immersive media. We will always aim to provide clear descriptions of any sensory content in advance. Please contact us if you have sensory sensitivities and we will advise on suitability or make adjustments where possible.

This website

We aim to make this website accessible and usable for as many people as possible. We are working towards meeting the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at level AA.

Current measures include:

  • Descriptive alt text on images
  • Sufficient colour contrast for text
  • Resizable text without loss of content
  • Keyboard-navigable links and buttons
  • Semantic HTML structure with headings, landmarks and clear link text

What we are still improving

We are aware the following areas need further work and are improving them:

  • Physical access details for the venue (step-free routes, accessible toilet locations)
  • Captions and transcripts for video content
  • Full keyboard accessibility audit of all interactive elements
