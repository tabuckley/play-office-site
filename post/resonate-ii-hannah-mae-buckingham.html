Resonate (ii) — Hannah Mae Buckingham

25 August 2025

Resonate artist Hannah Mae Buckingham reflects on their process — now that the exhibition In Touch, In Ruin is open at Aspex Portsmouth, here is a glimpse into the making.

salt – silt – silk – kiss

One of the first things I made during this residency was a short sound piece. It consists of the four words 'salt – silt – silk – kiss', layered with field recordings of the waves and sea from the area around the Hard. It loops in an endless cycle, creating an almost ambient echo. The sound piece uses a directional speaker, which creates an immersive and intimate experience by channelling sound in a specific direction.

Soft Engineering

To make a bed of silt, I searched for 'bed of silt' images online and came across an environmental object called a 'silt mat' — a natural and effective soft engineering solution for restoring balance and managing sediment pollution in channels. I knew I wanted to recreate this idea as a pillow that could be felt, touched, rested on — a symbol of the shared sleeping space of two men on board the Mary Rose, a final resting place after it sank.

I used banana fibre yarn (bananas being one of the largest imports in Portsmouth) to create the woven grid. I made an internal pillow with deadstock organza filled with sheep's wool stuffing, adding dried lavender — a herb used within Tudor burials. The borders are recycled sari-silk yarn, hand-stitched onto the weave. The whole process was done by hand.
