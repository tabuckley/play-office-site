Resonate (ii) — Touring Open Studio: XR and Heritage

In 2025, Play Office travelled to Cairo to present Resonate II at Medrar for Contemporary Art — sharing a live snapshot of a Portsmouth-based programme in motion. This was not a finished exhibition; it was an open studio, a working process, and an invitation to test how a project shifts when it meets a new city, a new audience, and new questions.

The Mary Rose Archive

Three artists explored the archives of the Mary Rose Trust — King Henry VIII's flagship that sank in 1545 and was raised from the seabed in 1982. Over 19,000 artefacts were recovered, offering an unusually detailed picture of life aboard a Tudor warship. For us, this archive is not only a historical record — it is a living material, dense with labour, conflict, survival, care, and technology.

Reframing Heritage

As Thomas Buckley writes: “In a city which holds its past as closely as Portsmouth does, it’s vital we open space for artists to butt heads with what we think our past tells us about who we can be now. By boldly exploring technologies which feel almost like science fiction, we can open up a potential space for re-invention and reinvigoration.”

Bringing a Portsmouth-rooted archive project to Cairo opened new space around the politics of preservation — who the archive serves, what is remembered, what is erased, and what it means to keep history open rather than sealed. Instead of presenting heritage as a fixed narrative, Resonate II presented it as a site of ongoing negotiation.

Thank you to Medrar for hosting and for creating the conditions for genuine exchange — where process is valued, and where questions stay open.
