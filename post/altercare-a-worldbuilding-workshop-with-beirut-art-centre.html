AlterCare: A Worldbuilding Workshop with Beirut Art Centre

What happens when you treat an ecology, not as a backdrop, but as the engine of a world?

At Beirut Art Centre, Play Office ran an XR workshop with artist and Resonate alumni Lara Kobeissi, inviting participants to explore ecologies, worldbuilding, and emerging technologies as one connected creative practice. The goal was not to learn software in isolation — it was to build a shared pipeline where lived environments, material traces, and embodied movement could become the foundations of immersive worlds.

The Process

Over the course of the workshop, participants developed speculative XR scenes by moving through a complete process: capturing reality, transforming it into 3D material, animating presence, and assembling worlds with intention. We used AI motion capture to generate animation from simple video recordings, and photogrammetry to capture objects and surfaces from the surrounding environment.

What Was Made

By the end, the room was full of early-stage worlds — hybrid environments where scanned materials became landmarks, movement became narrative, and ecology operated as a design constraint rather than a theme. More importantly, the workshop produced a shared technical vocabulary, peer-to-peer problem solving, and a repeatable workflow participants can adapt to their own practice.

This workshop was facilitated and funded by the British Council Lebanon. A huge thank you to Beirut Art Centre for hosting, to Lara Kobeissi for co-leading with clarity and care, and to every participant.
